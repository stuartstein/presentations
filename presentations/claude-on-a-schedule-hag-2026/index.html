the easy part

Two ways to schedule it.

Cowork · Scheduled Task

Runs on your machine

It can reach your local files. The catch: it needs your laptop awake and the Claude desktop app open. The app can keep your computer from sleeping to fire the prompt — or it queues the run for next time you log in.

Claude Code · Routine

Runs in the cloud

It lives on Anthropic's servers, so your computer can be closed and it still runs. The trade-off: it can't touch anything on your local machine, and there may be a cap on how many you can run.

which one

Local for you,
cloud for the team.

non-critical

Personal & low-stakes

I run these as Scheduled Tasks in Cowork. If a run slips because my laptop was closed, no harm done.

must run

Team-facing output

Anything that posts to the team belongs in a cloud Routine, so it fires at the same time whether or not my laptop is open.

Either way, the shape is the same — you give it a title, instructions to run, and a cadence.

the hard part

Scheduling is easy.
The prompt is the craft.

If it runs every day, the output has to be reliably good. That's what earns more care up front — a throwaway prompt won't survive a hundred runs.

getting started

Three ways to get unstuck.

lazy

Just ask

“Write me a skill to draft my standup daily. Ask me everything you need to know.” Fully endorsed — it's the fastest way to start.

lazy + rigorous

Make it grill you

Add a pre-built skill like /grill-me so Claude interrogates the plan before writing a line.

from examples

Start from the past

“Read my last two weeks of standups first, then ask what's missing.” Claude imitates good examples well.

All three get you surprisingly far. The catch with just ask: Claude is eager, so it tends to under-ask before diving in.

when you have opinions

Or brain-dump first,
then fill the gaps.

When you already know what you want, lead with it. Dump your specifics, then have Claude question you to surface everything you left out.

the prompt

One brief, everything wired in.

prompt.md

Help me write a prompt Claude can trigger daily to draft my standup.

Match my past standups in the #product “Daily standup time” thread — Claude learns best from good examples.

Read my last five standups, paying special attention to yesterday's “what I'll do today.”

Pull from Google Calendar, my Slack DMs / mentions / links, Notion & Drive docs, the occasional Gmail, and the “Stu's Meetings” Notion database. Skip weekends and days I'm out of office.

Output a Slack message to me, linking each item to its doc, Figma file, or thread. Hard-code the channel and database IDs so it runs fast.

anatomy

What a good recurring
prompt carries.

1 · intent

Stated twice

Open and close with what you actually want out of it.

2 · examples

Something to imitate

Point at past work so it matches your format and voice.

3 · output

Format & destination

Say what it produces and exactly where it lands.

4 · sources

Where to look

Every place the data lives, and what to look for in each.

5 · guardrails

What to skip

Weekends, OOO days, noise that shouldn't show up.

6 · questions

“Ask me first”

Force clarifying questions before it ever runs.
